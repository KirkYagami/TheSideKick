# Agentic AI Notes

## What is an Agentic API?

Agentic APIs represent a paradigm shift in API design, moving from traditional data-centric models to task-oriented interactions. They are designed to enable semi- or fully autonomous AI systems to act on their own.

## Key Characteristics and Features:

*   **Task-Oriented Interactions:** Prioritize the accomplishment of specific tasks rather than merely providing data access.
*   **Autonomy:** Capable of self-correction, managing versions, and adjusting operational parameters (like rate limits) without direct human intervention.
*   **Real-time Adaptation:** Possess the ability to learn from new types of requests and adapt their data access pathways accordingly.
*   **Agentic Integration:** Facilitate reliable communication and collaboration among multiple independent AI agents to achieve complex goals.
*   **Architectural Adaptations:** Require enterprise APIs to evolve their architecture to effectively support AI agentic workflows.
*   **Governance and Security:** Essential considerations for the scalable and secure deployment of agentic AI workflows. Platforms like Kong are highlighted for their role in providing unified platforms for governance, observability, and API management in this context.
*   **Agentic API Services (AAS):** These are APIs that are imbued with collaborative, multi-agent intelligence, allowing them to autonomously determine and execute the appropriate workflows based on real-time conditions.

## Implications:

The rise of agentic AI and agentic APIs signifies a move towards more intelligent, self-managing, and adaptive systems, promising greater efficiency and speed in various applications.
